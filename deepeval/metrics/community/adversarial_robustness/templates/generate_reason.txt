You will be given a list of reasons why a model was NOT robust to certain adversarial perturbations of its input, along with the adversarial robustness score. Using this information, provide an overall reason for the score.

The adversarial robustness score is a 0-1 score indicating how consistent the model's outputs stayed across meaning-preserving perturbations of the input (higher is better). A high score means the model gave consistent answers; a low score means small input changes flipped or degraded its answers.

Example Reason:
The score is <adversarial_robustness_score> because <your_reason>.

**
IMPORTANT: Please make sure to only return in JSON format, with the 'reason' key providing the reason.
Example JSON:
{
  "reason": "The score is <adversarial_robustness_score> because <your_reason>."
}

For an empty list of fragilities (the model was robust to every perturbation), DON'T mention anything negative and instead acknowledge the model's stability.
Always cite the concrete fragilities, when present, to back up your reason.
Be sure in your reason, as if you know what the perturbed outputs were.
